01 Executive summary One page · Four takeaways

Each of the four summarizes a section of this pre-read. If only one page is read, this is the one.

I.

Three structural gaps stall AI, not one.

Quality is validated after decisions, not before. Documents are invisible to structured DQ. No single trust score rolls up per data product. All three must be closed together.

Assure closes all three in one platform.

05 Product architecture Exhibit 4 of 6

Three modules — structured, unstructured, pipeline — share one rule catalog and roll up to one trust score.

Modules are independent (license one, two, or all three) but compose. Each is shown below with its Aflac-relevant application.

Exhibit 4 · The three modules and how they interlock
SDQ · Module 01
Structured Data Quality
Validates tables and warehouses the way an auditor would — systematically, versioned, and in place.
What it checks
  • Completeness — nulls, required fields
  • Validity — types, enums, formats
  • Consistency — cross-field invariants
  • Uniqueness — exact + fuzzy dedup
  • Timeliness — freshness vs. cadence
  • Accuracy — cross-source agreement
Aflac application
Auto-adjudication models depend on policy-status, effective-date, and event-date being mutually consistent. SDQ gates the record before it reaches the decisioning step — with every rule change versioned.
UDQ · Module 02
Unstructured Data Quality
Treats PDFs, scans, and free-text as first-class data — with document-type-aware rules.
What it checks
  • Capture — OCR confidence, integrity
  • Completeness — required fields by type
  • Semantic coherence — content matches type
  • Extraction validity — vs. source of truth
  • Duplicate & version chains
  • PHI / PII / PCI exposure
Aflac application
A hospitalization claim arrives with attestation + discharge summary. UDQ gates OCR quality, verifies required fields, confirms the document is semantically what it claims to be, and flags PHI surfaces.
PDQ · Module 03
Proactive Data Quality
Embeds quality enforcement inside pipelines — one rule, many enforcement points.
Severity → action
  • CRITICAL — block the batch
  • HIGH — route to review queue
  • MEDIUM — annotate with warning flag
  • LOW — log only, no flow impact
  • Per-environment overrides
  • Full pipeline lineage out of the box
Aflac application
A CRITICAL rule blocks the batch; a HIGH rule routes one record to a reviewer; the remaining 97% continues to auto-adjudication without added latency. The audit log shows exactly which rule fired, where.
Read together. The modules are MECE — they address structured, unstructured, and pipeline surfaces without overlap. The shared rail ensures rule reuse and score aggregation across all three.
06 The governance artifact Exhibit 5 of 6

A composite trust score per data product — one number, consumable as a gate.

An illustrative breakdown of how Assure computes a trust score. Weighted dimensions prevent a single critical failure from being masked by strong overall performance, and prevent strong overall performance from being masked by a single noisy dimension.

Exhibit 5 · Illustrative trust score composition — claims_stp_inputs_certified
Why this matters. The trust score is the artifact regulators, auditors, and model-risk reviewers already ask about — produced as a byproduct of operation, not as an after-the-fact reconstruction. An AI pipeline can refuse to run against an uncertified product; an auditor can trace any decision back to the posture at the moment the decision was made.
