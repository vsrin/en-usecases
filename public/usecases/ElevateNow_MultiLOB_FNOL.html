Architecture

Facts. Intelligence. Evidence.

Three layers, consistently applied across every line of business. The framework doesn't change. The intelligence focus does.

Layer 1
Facts

Structured extraction from the FNOL document — claimant, incident, policy, coverage, parties, dates. Normalized into a consistent schema regardless of how the narrative was written or what information was omitted.

Layer 2
Intelligence

LOB-specific analysis applied to those facts — liability theory, coverage determination, indicator scoring, reserve calculation, authority routing. The analysis that previously required an experienced adjuster with the file open in front of them.

Layer 3
Evidence

Every conclusion is traceable to a verbatim quote from the source document. The platform does not infer without a citation. Every indicator, every liability determination, every risk transfer recommendation shows its source text.

Four Lines of Business

The intelligence priority shifts by LOB. The architecture does not.

What matters most on Day 1 is different for Property, Auto, GL, and Workers Comp. ElevateNow encodes the right analytical focus for each line — not a generic extraction layer.

Property

Signals — what this claim is actually about

A kitchen fire, an unknown cause, a Fire Marshal on scene. The platform runs 14 indicator patterns against the FNOL text. When three fire — arson/incendiary, displaced occupants, recent limit increase — the consequence is immediate: SIU referral, evidence preservation, EUO. The adjuster under volume pressure is most likely to miss the indicator buried in the last paragraph. The platform surfaces it before the file is opened.

Day 1 priority: indicators → routing → coverage dispute identification
Auto

Recovery — who else is on the hook

A standard rear-end collision has two recovery targets when the at-fault driver was making a delivery. The platform identifies the employer behind the driver from three phrases in the document: "COMMERCIAL VEHICLE," "Delivery Driver," "making a delivery." Two subrogation theories generated: personal policy and commercial fleet. Evidence preservation checklist built. The recovery window is still open on Day 1 — it won't be on Day 30.

Day 1 priority: employer/commercial vehicle identification → dual recovery strategy
General Liability

Risk transfer — who owes the insured a defense

A slip-and-fall at a commercial premises has a snow removal contractor mid-treatment when it happened. The platform finds the MSA, quotes Section 10 — broad-form indemnification — and answers the anti-indemnity question before the examiner makes a call. The IL Construction Contract Indemnification Act does not apply to snow removal. The indemnification holds. 40% of a $524,000 exposure shifts to ProClear before the file is assigned.

Day 1 priority: contractor MSA → indemnification enforceability → tender strategy
Workers Compensation

Compensability — did this happen at work, in the course of employment

The platform interrogates AOE/COE from the FNOL text before the examiner opens the file. Employer defenses are screened (intoxication, horseplay, willful misconduct). Jurisdiction-specific benefit tables applied for TTD/PTD/PPD. OSHA trigger detected. Third-party equipment or premises liability identified for subrogation screening. The examiner validates the determination — they don't build it from scratch.

Day 1 priority: compensability verdict → jurisdiction benefits → third-party recovery screen
