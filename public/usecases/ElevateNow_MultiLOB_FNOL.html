How It Works

9-Stage Intelligence Pipeline

ElevateNow processes every FNOL through nine analytical stages, each applying specific institutional knowledge and business rules. Results are deterministic and referenceable—the same inputs always produce the same analysis with complete documentation of reasoning.

Stage 0: Data Extraction

Extracts structured information from FNOL documents (PDFs, emails, transcripts). For Auto claims, determines required sub-files (collision, bodily injury per party, PIP, rental).

→

Stage 1: Jurisdiction

Applies state-specific rules: Workers Comp benefit rates and filing deadlines, Auto fault systems (comparative vs. contributory negligence), no-fault PIP requirements.

Stage 2: Knowledge Retrieval

Identifies relevant institutional knowledge based on claim characteristics—jurisdiction-specific case law, coverage interpretations, benefit calculation rules.

→

Stage 3: Primary Analysis

Workers Comp: Compensability assessment with confidence score. Auto: Liability determination with fault allocation. Both produce documented reasoning for their conclusions.

Stage 4: Fraud Detection

Screens for sensitive indicators: fraud patterns, attorney involvement timing, delayed reporting, inconsistent statements, phantom passengers (auto), staged collisions.

→

Stage 5: Subrogation

Evaluates recovery potential against five liability theories. Identifies responsible third parties, estimates recovery amount, documents evidence preservation requirements.

Stage 6: Reserve Calculation

Workers Comp: Benefit calculation based on average weekly wage and jurisdiction rates. Auto: Damage estimates plus bodily injury exposure per claimant. Produces tiered scenarios.

→

Stage 7: Authority Routing

Evaluates confidence scores, fraud indicators, reserve amounts, and subrogation potential against explicit thresholds. Routes to auto-accept, senior review, or investigation with documented logic.

Stage 8: Intelligence Report

Assembles all upstream analysis into a structured briefing: executive summary, recommended actions, compliance deadlines, complete decision trail with knowledge references.

How the System Handles Complexity

The platform has been validated against reference claims representing the full spectrum: WC chemical exposure with third-party equipment liability, Auto multi-vehicle collision with municipal signal malfunction, and cross-LOB subrogation scenarios where Auto collision spawns WC claims for commercial drivers.

Chemical Exposure → Equipment Subrogation

Scenario: TCE vapor release, 3 affected employees, one hospitalized. Equipment seal failure on degreasing unit serviced 32 days prior by manufacturer.

Analysis: Catastrophic indicator (hospitalization) + OSHA trigger + third-party equipment liability identified → manufacturer PVS added to subrogation screen. Evidence preservation checklist auto-generated: gasket, service records, maintenance contract, 311 reports.

Auto Liability — Critical

Multi-Vehicle Collision → Municipal Liability

Scenario: 3-vehicle intersection T-bone. Traffic signal malfunctioned for 24+ hours. City had 311 complaints but dispatched no repair crew. 2 hospitalized, attorney retained within 24 hours.

Analysis: 7 sub-files spawned (PD-COLL, PIP, BI × 2 attorney-rep'd, RENTAL, SUBRO-CITY, SUBRO-conventional). Municipal liability flagged via FL Statute §316.0745. Comparative fault allocated: at-fault driver 60–70%, City 30–40%, our insured 0–15%.
