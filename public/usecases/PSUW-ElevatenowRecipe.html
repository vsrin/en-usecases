Public Sector Risk Is Documented. Just Not Where Underwriters Look.

Counties and municipalities are unusual risks — they operate jails, water treatment plants, law enforcement agencies, transit systems, and healthcare facilities. The documentation is largely public: budgets, board minutes, environmental compliance records, court filings. The problem isn't that the information doesn't exist. It's that reading it at scale is humanly impossible.

DISCLOSURE GAP

What Was Submitted vs. What the Record Shows

Board meeting minutes are public record and contain board-approved claim settlements, reserve fund transfers for litigation, and carrier complaint discussions. Most applications never surface these. The gap between the submitted loss history and the board-approved settlement register is a disclosure problem, not a data problem.

SOCIAL INFLATION

Jury Awards Vary by County, Not Just by State

State-level tort reform data misses the variance that actually matters. Plaintiff attorney concentration, population density, and actual circuit court filing rates differ dramatically county to county. An account in Wayne County, Michigan carries a fundamentally different LEL exposure than one in Keweenaw — same state, different universe.

SIGNAL BURIED

The Pattern Is Negligence. The Incident Is Just the Evidence.

A single deferred maintenance mention in a board meeting is a budget choice. The same infrastructure complaint raised at six consecutive meetings with no resolution creates a documented negligence trail. Pattern detection across the minutes record is where the real exposure signal lives.

NEW
Meeting Minutes Intelligence — 10 Signal Categories

Meeting minutes are the entity's unfiltered self-portrait. Every other data source is the entity presenting itself to the outside world. Minutes are the entity talking to itself — and that's where the real risk profile lives. The tool discovers, downloads, and reads board meeting PDFs from official government domains, extracting 10 signal categories that no other underwriting intelligence source reaches.

Signal 01

Program Stability Score

Annual re-bid authorizations, broker changes, board-approved coverage reductions. Classifies as Stable (same carrier 3+ years), Shopping (annual bids), or Distressed (coverage reductions, pool exits, self-insured discussions).

Signal 02

Shadow Settlement Register

Every claim settlement approved by board vote — dollar amount, type, department. Cross-checkable against stated loss history. A board-approved $1.2M in settlements against a submitted "no losses over $50K" is a disclosure problem surfaced before quoting begins.

Signal 03

Recurring Issue Tracker

Topics that appear across multiple meetings in the review period. A single deferred maintenance mention is a budget choice. The same issue raised at six consecutive meetings with no resolution action creates a documented negligence trail a plaintiff's attorney will use.

Signal 04

Exposure Change Log

New construction approvals, property acquisitions, fleet purchases — capital and property changes that affect coverage but haven't yet been reported to the carrier. Mid-term coverage gaps identified before they become post-loss surprises.

Signal 05

LEL Governance Block

Use of force policy changes (proactive vs. reactive), body camera policy votes, consent decree or DOJ engagement mentions, jail/detention conditions. Dedicated law enforcement liability signals, not buried in general legal findings.

Signal 06

Financial Stress Composite

Short-term borrowing resolutions, fund balance warnings, tax revenue shortfalls, pension/OPEB discussions, grant dependency risks. Financial stress is the upstream predictor of deferred maintenance, reduced risk management, and aggressive claim disputes.

Signal 07

Closed Session Pattern

Agenda descriptions of closed sessions (legally public even if content is not). Volume and subject distribution — litigation, personnel, labor, real property — reveals active problems being managed quietly. 18 closed sessions in a year reads differently than 3.

Signal 08

Contractor & Vendor Risk

Low-bid awards with on-record objections, large infrastructure contracts without COI discussion, performance disputes and bond calls. Board-record objections create future liability exposure admissible in negligence claims.

Signal 09

Cyber & Technology Signals

Large system implementations (ERP, payroll, 911 dispatch) during which cyber exposure spikes, IT vendor contracts with data custody implications, any breach or incident references in session agendas.

Signal 10

Employee & HR Signals

Safety committee activity and budget trends, return-to-work program references (WC claim management indicator), FMLA/ADA accommodation disputes (EPL exposure), union grievances and labor negotiations.

PROPRIETARY
Social Inflation Proxy (SIP) Engine

Social inflation — the tendency of jury awards and settlement values to increase faster than economic inflation — is not uniform across a state. It varies by county, driven by population density, plaintiff-favorable judicial venues, and concentration of plaintiff law firms. Most carriers apply state-level tort reform flags or zip code premium loads. Neither captures the county-level variance that actually determines litigation exposure. The SIP Engine makes this quantifiable.

County-Level, Not State-Level

Wayne County, Michigan (Detroit) carries a J_scao score of 1.000 based on 15,871 circuit court filings. Keweenaw County scores 0.168 based on 4 filings. Same state. Same tort reform environment. Underwriting them identically on LEL or auto liability is a pricing error, not a conservative choice.

Factor D

Population Density

Higher density correlates with more claims, larger jury pools drawn from urban environments, and media attention on high-value verdicts.

Factor J

Judicial Climate

Actual SCAO circuit court civil filing rates per county — not estimated, not synthesized. Sourced directly from Michigan Supreme Court Administrative Office data.

Factor L

Legal Presence

Plaintiff law firm concentration and legal services density at the county level. Where attorneys are concentrated, claims get filed and pursued more aggressively.

INTELLIGENCE
External Risk Intelligence — 13 Domains, Coverage-Line Organized

Thirteen search domains scan public domain sources including news articles, government filings, court records, and regulatory announcements. Five domains activate conditionally based on detected departments — law enforcement intelligence only runs when a Sheriff or Police budget is present. All findings are entity-validated (this county, not a similarly named one), credibility-scored, and organized by coverage line rather than search domain. The result is not a list of 30 web findings — it's a disclosure cross-check against the submitted application and an active incident tracker by coverage line.

ENVIRONMENTAL
Environmental Compliance — PFAS Against 2024 EPA MCL

When water or wastewater departments are detected in the budget, the system determines whether the county directly owns and operates the water system — or whether a separate utility authority holds that liability. For county-operated systems, PFAS compound levels are checked against 2024 EPA Maximum Contaminant Levels (PFOA: 4 ppt, PFOS: 4 ppt, PFHxS: 10 ppt). Compliance status, active violation proceedings, and remediation history feed directly into the underwriting assessment and pollution liability trigger analysis.

Governance by Design

Every Conclusion Is Verifiable. That's the Architecture.

AI assistance earns underwriter trust only when every conclusion can be traced to evidence. Auditability is not a feature added after the system was built — it is the structural requirement the system was designed around.

Hotspot Citations

Every claim in the narrative links to its source. "14% expansion in Detention" leads directly to the budget line item, classification confidence, and matching rule. Nothing is asserted without a traceable origin.

Supplemental Logic Chains

Each required form shows a 3-step reasoning trace: Step 1 Detection, Step 2 Risk Association, Step 3 Rule Application. Auditors trace the full path from raw data to the supplemental request — documented, not recalled.

Deterministic Pre-Analysis

Escalation tier is computed deterministically from rule-based signals before any LLM synthesis runs. The LLM can raise the tier but cannot lower it. Governance constraints are architectural, not instructional.

Source Quality Scoring

Findings carry credibility scores. A federal court filing is weighted differently from a local newspaper article. Low-confidence findings surface with explicit review flags — they don't disappear into a filtered list the underwriter never sees.
